Services provided by Somerset West Night Shelter

Overnight Accommodation
  • Overnight Shelter: Safe and secure accommodation for individuals who are homeless or in need of temporary housing.
  • Emergency Shelter: Immediate, short-term shelter for those facing crisis situations.
  • Transitional Housing: Longer-term accommodation for individuals progressing through recovery or reintegration programs.
Meals
  • Daily Meals: Nutritious meals (breakfast, lunch, and dinner) are provided to residents.
  • Food Parcels: Sometimes food parcels are distributed to non-residents in need, depending on resources.
Counseling and Emotional Support
  • Individual Counseling: Emotional and psychological support is available through social workers or counselors.
  • Group Therapy: Peer support groups or recovery programs may be organized to provide shared experiences and support.
  • Substance Abuse Support: Help for individuals struggling with addiction, including referrals to rehabilitation services.
Social Work Services
  • Case Management: Social workers help clients develop individualized plans for rehabilitation and reintegration.
  • Family Reunification: Assistance in reconnecting homeless individuals with their families, when appropriate.
  • Documentation Support: Assistance with acquiring ID cards, birth certificates, and other essential documents.
Employment and Job Placement Services
  • Job Search Assistance: Guidance in finding job opportunities, including résumé writing and interview preparation.
  • Work Placements: Partnering with local businesses or organizations to provide temporary or permanent employment opportunities for residents.
  • Volunteer Work: Opportunities within the shelter for residents to gain work experience and build confidence.
Health Services
  • Basic Health Care: Access to medical care and first aid for minor injuries and illnesses.
  • Referrals to Clinics and Hospitals: Referring residents to specialized medical services when necessary.
  • Mental Health Support: Support for individuals suffering from mental health conditions, including referrals to mental health professionals.
Clothing Distribution
  • Clothing Donations: Providing donated clothing, shoes, and blankets to residents.
  • Seasonal Clothing: Ensuring that individuals have access to warm clothes during winter months or appropriate clothing for job interviews.
