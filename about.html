About Somerset West Night-Shelter

Overview of our Night Shelter

The Somerset West Night Shelter was established in 1988 and is situated at 71 Church Street, Somerset West, on land leased from the City of Cape Town. Since its inception, the shelter has been dedicated to addressing homelessness in the Helderberg District, providing a safe haven and essential services to those in need.

Facilities and Capacity:

  • Accommodation: The shelter is designed to accommodate up to 42 men and 12 women.
  • Male and Female Dormitories: Separate sleeping areas to ensure privacy and safety.
  • Full Bathrooms: Equipped with warm showers for personal hygiene.
  • Communal Areas: Spaces designated for eating, relaxation, and entertainment.
  • Kitchen: Facilitates the preparation and distribution of meals to residents.

The shelter's mission revolves around social rehabilitation and community support by:

  • Providing Temporary Shelter: Offering a safe and secure environment for homeless adults.
  • Social Rehabilitation: Assisting individuals with their physical, spiritual, and economic needs to foster self-reliance and reintegration into society.
  • Raising Community Awareness: Educating the public about the causes and challenges of homelessness, and encouraging community involvement in alleviating these issues.
  • Developing Support Centers: Establishing additional centers to support the homeless across the Helderberg District.
  • Supporting Social Welfare Networks: Enhancing structures that address the welfare needs of homeless adults.

In the winter, the shelter adds more beds to accommodate the higher demand. In addition to providing housing, the shelter assists individuals with applying for identification documents.

As a registered non-profit organization (NPO) and Public Benefit Organization (PBO), the shelter's goals include reducing homelessness, raising community awareness, and partnering with businesses through South Africa's B-BBEE program to secure funding and support.
